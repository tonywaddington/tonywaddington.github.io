7 Leighton Ave

Fee simple

This is a fee simple (freehold) property. Owners of fee simple properties have the right to sell, trade, gift or bequeath the property in a will. If the owner meets all current legislation, regulations and rules, they also have the rights to subdivide, build, lease, mortgage and register interests on the title (more on this later in the module).

Fee simple estates potentially last forever (in contrast with some types of title, which are of limited duration).

Company share

This is a company share apartment. Here’s how this operates.

  • A company is formed, which becomes the land-owning entity. It doesn’t trade in any goods or services, because it’s just a means of property ownership.
  • Shareholders have either a lease or licence to occupy one of the apartments. The lease or licence may contain conditions, for example, apartments cannot be rented, or no pets. Many company share schemes have a method of screening potential shareholders, giving the property a sense of exclusivity.
  • Each shareholder pays a levy to cover their share of the costs of the property, such as rates, insurance, maintenance, security and lighting for common areas, and often a ‘sinking fund’, which is effectively a savings plan for when large and expensive maintenance requirements arise.

Leasehold

This house is on leasehold land. Leasehold properties are less common than fee simple and they can be more complicated. With leasehold estates, there are two parties involved: the lessor (the land owner) and a lessee (who has an agreement to lease the property). The lessee gets the legal right to exclusive possession of the land for a certain period, in exchange for a payment. The lessee pays the normal property expenses such as council rates, property insurance and maintenance.

15A Harper Road

Cross lease

This is a cross lease property. You can see on the Certificate of Title that there are two flats on the property.

A cross lease title is a combination of freehold ownership and long-term leases. Each owner owns an undivided share of the land, and leases their flat from each of the other owners as tenants in common. Each owner has rights to the undivided piece of land and possession rights to a specific part of the property (for example, their flat, parking area and garden).

In addition to the Certificate of Title, there will be a lease document, which contains a set of covenants (rules). For example, some cross lease properties don't allow tenants or pets.

15 Lime Crescent

Stratum estate

This unit is a stratum estate (also called unit title). This is the most common form of ownership for apartments, home units, townhouses and small multi-unit business premises. The whole property, including all land, buildings and other structures, makes up the stratum estate. Each owner has a separate title, usually in fee simple (but sometimes in leasehold) for their strata unit. Together, all owners are the body corporate, which owns the common property.
