What would be a better option for John?

Option 2:

  • 1 cup of tea with whole milk and 2 tsp white sugar
  • 2 pieces of toast (white bread) with butter, jam and honey

Incorrect

Correct

This is the ideal breakfast alternative, as sugar has been removed from his coffee, porridge is made with wholegrain oats, and nutrient-rich fruit (blueberries) has been added as an alternative to sugar.

Lunch

John's current lunch is:

  • 2 luncheon and cheese sandwiches (white bread)
  • 1 medium banana

What would be a better option for John?

Option 1:

  • 1 steak and cheese pie and 1 chocolate muffin from bakery
  • 1 apple

Incorrect

This is not an ideal lunch alternative, as a pie has a high fat and energy content with low fibre. The chocolate muffin also has a high fat and sugar content with low fibre.

Option 2:

  • 1 ham, cheese, avocado, spinach and tomato wrap (1 large mixed grain wrap, with ham off the bone and Edam cheese)
  • 1 mandarin

Correct

This is the ideal lunch alternative, as a wrap has a lower carbohydrate content and higher fibre. The avocado provides healthy fat (monounsaturated) and ham off the bone has fewer preservatives. A mandarin has lower sugar content than a banana, so this is a great alternative.

Option 3:

  • 2 streaky bacon, lettuce, cheese and mayonnaise sandwiches (white bread)
  • 1 banana

Incorrect

This is not an ideal lunch, as streaky bacon and mayonnaise have high fat contents. Including a piece of fruit is great. However, bananas have a higher sugar content than other fruits like raspberries or watermelon.
