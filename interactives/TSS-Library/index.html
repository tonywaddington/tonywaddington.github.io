What's in the library?
Dictionary
Information type:

Provides definitions of words. Like encyclopedias, they can either be general or topic-specific.

Useful for:

Defining key terms for research.

Advantages:

Useful for clarifying your understanding of key terms.

Disadvantages:

Do not give details or explanations about ideas.

Newspaper
Information type:

A collection of articles about current events, usually published daily.

Useful for:

Finding current information about international, national and local events / Finding editorials, commentaries, expert or popular opinions.

Advantages:

Up-to-date information.

Disadvantages:

Quickly becomes dated.

E-books
Information type:

A book-length publication in electronic form.

Useful for:

Finding information to put your topic in context / Finding historical information or summaries on a topic.

Advantages:

Easy to access and find information within the e-book. / Ability to select, copy, highlight and annotate text / Easier to update than printed books.

Disadvantages:

Needs to be read on an electronic device / Some self-publishing means e-books aren’t always as rigorously checked as printed books.

Encyclopedia
Information type:

Collections of short, factual entries, either general or topic-specific.

Useful for:

Finding background information on a topic / Finding key ideas, important dates or concepts.

Advantages:

Provide background information as a useful starting place for further research.

Disadvantages:

Can become outdated.

Note: Wikipedia is an internet encyclopaedia, to which anyone can add information. There’s no guarantee information is accurate, but it can be a good starting place to gain an overview of a topic.

Books
Information type:

Books cover virtually any topic. For research purposes, you’ll probably be looking for books drawing together the information on your topic.

Useful for:

Finding information to put your topic in context / Finding historical information or summaries on a topic.

Advantages:

Accurate and credible information that has been edited.

Disadvantages:

Information can become dated, as books are difficult to update.

Magazine
Information type:

A collection of articles and images on topics of popular interest.

Useful for:

Finding information or opinions about popular culture.

Advantages:

Timely information about current events.

Disadvantages:

Information can become dated / Written for a general audience.

Journal
Information type:

A collection of articles written by experts, usually reviewed to ensure they are of high quality. Articles cover very specific topics.

Useful for:

Conducting scholarly research.

Advantages:

Credible and detailed information about specific topics.

Disadvantages:

Information can become dated / Not easily accessible.
