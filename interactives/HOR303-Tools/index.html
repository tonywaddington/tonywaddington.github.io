Pruning saw

There are many different types of pruning saw.

Easier to use than most other pruning tools.

Very sharp teeth.

Generally used on small branches or those bigger than 2.5 cm.

Close

Secateurs

Used for cutting small branches up to 1 cm and buds or soft stems.

Cause little damage to plant if well maintained.

Two types, anvil and parrot-beaked.

Used with cutting blade at top of cut.

Close

Loppers

Two types, anvil and parrot-beaked.

Parrot-beak tend to make a cleaner cut.

Long handles increase leverage.

Used on branches 1 cm to 4cm thick.

Close

Pole pruner

Good for cutting high branches.

Use a rope to operate the blade.

Good for cutting horizontal branches.

Used on branches 2 to 3 cm thick.

Close

Shears

Cuts back shoots and tips.

Not selective on what is cut.

Creates an even appearance across a large area.
