OP Christmas

HTML5 and JS interactive Christmas greetings from Open Polytechnic of New Zealand.

2015

An interactive Christmas greeting from the Open Polytechnic of New Zealand. Instead of a more traditional printed Christmas card, we opted to create a fun digital Christmas card involving an interactive game. The game involves finding Santa and his friends who are hiding in a winter forest. The game was created using HTML5 and Javascript for a modern browsing experience for desktop, mobile and cross-browser users, complete with sound design. We also created a Kiwi summer time version for those who do not celebrate Christmas - a summer time picnic on the beach theme.

Work produced for and owned by The Open Polytechnic of New Zealand.

2016

Following the success of the the previous years holiday message, we wanted to take the interactive game experience to the next level in 2016. We upped the interactivity to create a browser-based game whereby the user moves an elf from side to side to catch gifts falling from Santaâ€™s magical gift wrapping machine. We also took the gamification to the next level, adding features such as earning points for catching gifts, extra points for catching stars and points taken away for catching coal. The game also gets progressively faster throughout the minute of play. As the game was built in HTML5 and Javascript, we could offer a totally modern browser experience, usable across all devices and browsers. We also created a custom form so that staff could send the card with a personalised message, and included links in the game for people to share it with friends or family on social media. We also created a Kiwi summer time version for those who do not celebrate Christmas - a summer time BBQ grab.

Work produced for and owned by The Open Polytechnic of New Zealand.
