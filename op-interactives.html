OP Interactive

Interactive design for New Zealand’s leading distance education provider.

The Open Polytechnic (OP) is New Zealand’s leading distance education provider, offering students flexible learning for over 70 years. The online delivery of courses offers students an integrated and innovative digital learning experience. My work with OP involved elevating course content by creating engaging, innovative, and interactive digital learning experiences. OP gave me the freedom to interpret briefs creatively and I was able to rapidly develop my digital design skill set. On this page you will find a selection of some of the interactive work I have produced for OP from some of my favourite projects. This work was all built in HTML5, CSS3 and JavaScript for a modern browser experience across all devices. Underlying all the work was a need to provide a seamless user experience for students.

Work produced for and owned by The Open Polytechnic of New Zealand.

Student Journey
Custom slideshow

This interactive was designed for Open Polytechnic staff and depicts a student’s journey from first engagement with the brand to graduation. I have used a number of visual techniques in this interactive to emphasise the idea of the student’s journey and the passing of time. These included designing the slideshow to rotate around a globe and transitioning the sky in each slide to reflect the passing of time between sunrise to sunset.

Real Estate
Interactive map

An interactive map created for a real estate course. To help students relate to the concepts they were learning about, a fictional town and characters were created, which acted as a narrative running throughout the course. This interactive incorporates features that would be well known to students. For example students can zoom in and move around the map to view the different property types, similar to Google Maps. Clicking on a property provides further information about it and the student is able to view the relevant certificate of title.

Interpreting body language
Drag and drop quiz

Drag and drop quiz designed for a health and wellbeing course. The quiz aimed to teach students how to interpret body language to facilitate more effective communication. Students had to match the correct interpretation of the body language to the respective image of a person.

Bone and muscle map
Interactive diagram

An interactive diagram created for an exercise course to teach students all the muscles and bones in the body. Students can toggle between maps for muscles and bones, and also flip the model between anterior and posterior views. Clicking on a body part reveals a modal with a zoomed-in diagram that names the muscles or bones for that part of the body.

John’s eating habits
Multi-choice quiz

Created for an exercise course to teach students about giving dietary advice to clients and help the client improve his or her eating habits. Characters were developed that would be present throughout the narrative of the course and to whom the course concepts would be applied to. In this particular interactive we meet the character John, who wants to improve his health so he can enjoy spending time with his grandkids. Students are able to look at John’s current eating habits and select improvements for each meal. Descriptions are given for why certain choices are either better or worse for John’s health.

IT personas
Infographics

This interactive explores the personas of different IT users. I created a simple tabbed interactive that animates between each profile. Each profile has key stats highlighted with icons and graphs to make the information easier to digest. I built a framework for creating the graphs using CSS to make sure they were fully responsive, and using Javascript to make them easily adjustable when there were a number of graphs on a page. As a result, graphs can be created and customised by simply adjusting the data attributes in the HTML file.

Health services
Interactive map

This interactive was created for a Tertiary Study Skills (foundation) course. It aims to educate students about the resources and support available in their area that might be of assistance throughout their education journey (e.g. libraries, financial support services, health services). The map of New Zealand is split into geographic regions and students are able to access information from a menu panel or, alternatively, by clicking on the map itself which will open a directory of relevant organisations. Users can also skip directly to a slide in the journey by using the menu.
